#1 Salmon loin

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Salmon loins - 4 units of 200 gram
Zucchini - 4 small units or a large one
Edible flowers -
Olive oil - 1 tablespoon
Salt - A pinch
Fish broth - 1 glass
White wine - 2 tablespoons
Parsley - 2 sprigs
Cornstarch - 1 teaspoon
  • Step 1: Start by preparing the sauce. To do this, wash the parsley sprigs well, dry them a little and put them in a small saucepan. Also add the fish soup and heat the saucepan until it boils for 4 minutes. When it is done, blend it with the hand mixer.
  • Step 2: In a small glass, pour the cornstarch with the wine and mix well. Then, pour it into the saucepan with the broth and heat for a couple of minutes, stirring constantly, until you achieve a thick sauce. Season with salt and pepper to your liking and reserve the broth.
  • Step 3: In a saucepan, heat water with a pinch of salt and, when it starts to boil, add the whole zucchini. Cook them for 3 minutes if they are small or a few minutes more if you use a large one. Drain them and set them aside.
  • Step 4: Season the salmon fillets with salt and cook them on a very hot griddle or non-stick frying pan, greased with oil, for about 2 minutes on each side.
  • Step 5: Put the fish loins on plates and distribute the zucchini cut into thin slices and some edible flowers on top. Pour the hot sauce around and serve.

Source: El Mueble

#2 Oven-baked risotto

  • Time: 10 mins to prep and 25 mins to cook
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Smoked bacon chopped into small pieces - 250g pack
Chopped onion - 1
Butter - 25g
White wine (optional) - half a glass
Risotto rice - 300g
Halved cherry tomatoes - 150g pack
hot chicken stock (from a cube is fine) - 700g
Grated parmesan cheese - 50g
  • Step 1: Heat oven to 200C/fan 180C/gas 6. Fry the bacon pieces in an ovenproof pan or casserole dish for 3-5 mins until golden and crisp. Stir in the onion and butter and cook for 3-4 mins until soft. Tip in the rice and mix well until coated. Pour over the wine if using and cook for 2 mins until absorbed.
  • Step 2: Add the cherry tomatoes and the hot stock, then give the rice a quick stir. Cover with a tightly fitting lid and bake for 18 mins until just cooked. Stir through most of the parmesan and serve sprinkled with the remainder.

Source: BBC Good Food

#3 Chocolate fudge cake

  • Time: 25 minutes to prep and 30 to cook_
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Sunflower oil 150g
Self-raising flour 175g
Cocoa powder 2 tablespoons
Bicarbonate of soda 1 tablespoon
Caster sugar 150g
Golden syrup 2 tablespoon
Lightly beaten large eggs 2
Semi-skimmed milk 150ml
Unsalted butter 100g
Icing sugar 225g
Cocoa powder 40g
Milk 2Â½ tablespoons
  • Step 1: Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4. Oil and line the base of two 18cm sandwich tins. Sieve the flour, cocoa powder and bicarbonate of soda into a bowl. Add the caster sugar and mix well.
  • Step 2: Make a well in the centre and add the golden syrup, eggs, sunflower oil and milk. Beat well with an electric whisk until smooth.
  • Step 3: Pour the mixture into the two tins and bake for 25-30 mins until risen and firm to the touch. Remove from oven, leave to cool for 10 mins before turning out onto a cooling rack.
  • Step 4: To make the icing, beat the unsalted butter in a bowl until soft. Gradually sieve and beat in the icing sugar and cocoa powder, then add enough of the milk to make the icing fluffy and spreadable.
  • Step 5: Sandwich the two cakes together with the butter icing and cover the sides and the top of the cake with more icing.

Source: BBC Good Food
